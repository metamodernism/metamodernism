Insight Strategies

Worthwhile dilemmas

Insight Strategies is inspired by the Oblique Strategies set of cards created by Brian Eno and Peter Schmidt, first published in 1975. Each card contained a brief instruction or remark intended to break creative deadlocks. The cards arose from the independent practices of Eno and Schmidt. Eno had been keeping a set of phrases pinned to the wall of his studio, reminders of strategies worth pursuing when lost in the studio. Schmidt had been using a similar set of sentences since 1970. When they compared notes, they found the overlap and decided to combine them into a single published deck. Five editions have been produced since 1975.

Insight Strategies are our inspirational set of practices behaviours from over 20 years of cultural anthropology and research experience. These are not strategic frameworks or toolkits, nor attempts to define or reify insights. They're merely snippets of how the mind works to make meaning from the observation of people and cultures.

How to use

Read them, apply them if you wish. Better still, use these as inspiration to create your own.
