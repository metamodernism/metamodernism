Oblique Strategies

Over one hundred worthwhile dilemmas

Oblique Strategies is a set of cards created by Brian Eno and Peter Schmidt, first published in 1975. Each card contains a brief instruction or remark intended to break creative deadlocks.

The cards arose from the independent practices of Eno and Schmidt. Eno had been keeping a set of phrases pinned to the wall of his studio, reminders of strategies worth pursuing when lost in the studio. Schmidt had been using a similar set of sentences since 1970.

When they compared notes, they found the overlap and decided to combine them into a single published deck. Five editions have been produced since 1975.

How to use

When you encounter a block — in music, design, writing, or any creative work — draw a card. Follow the instruction literally, or use it as a lateral prompt to shift your thinking.

Tap the card or press any key to draw a new strategy.
